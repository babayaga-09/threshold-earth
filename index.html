▶ How to Play

No science background needed. Just read, think, and decide. Each round takes about 60 seconds. Total playtime: ~10 minutes.

01
📰
Read the Situation

Each level opens with a real scenario grounded in actual events — a country's energy policy, a dying river system, a collapsing fishery. Read it carefully.

02
⚖️
Make the Call

Two policy options appear. Neither is labelled right or wrong. Both are defensible. Choose based on your values and judgement — exactly like a real decision maker.

03
🌍
See the Consequences

After you choose, the real-world consequences are revealed — backed by scientific sources. Earth's health rises or falls based on your decision.

04
📊
Track Earth's Health

The bar at the top is Earth's planetary health index. Decisions that protect boundaries keep it green. Decisions that breach them push it toward red.

05
🏁
Final Score

After all 9 boundaries, your Earth Health percentage determines the outcome — saved, struggling, or collapsed.

06
🔁
Replay & Reflect

Try the other option. See how different choices change outcomes. The point isn't to win — it's to understand the tradeoffs.

■ About This Project
EXHIBITION PLACARD
🌐
What is Threshold?

Threshold: Save Earth is an interactive decision simulation built for the End-Term Exhibition on Climate Change. It is grounded in the Planetary Boundaries framework — nine biophysical thresholds that define a safe operating space for humanity (Rockström et al., Nature, 2009; Steffen et al., Science, 2015). Each of the nine scenarios presents a real policy dilemma faced by actual governments, institutions, or international bodies. Your choices reflect the genuine tradeoffs at the heart of climate decision-making.

🎯
Objective

To make planetary boundaries feel personal and urgent — not abstract. By placing you inside the decision, you experience the weight of the tradeoffs that scientists, policymakers, and citizens face every day.

💡
Key Takeaway

There are rarely clean answers. The same decision that protects one boundary can breach another. Understanding that tension is more valuable than knowing the "right" answer.

🔬
Research Basis

All scenarios, statistics, and consequences are sourced from peer-reviewed journals and UN agency reports including IPCC AR6 (2021), IPBES Global Assessment (2019), WMO/UNEP (2022), WHO (2021), UN-Water (2023), and OECD (2022).

🛠️
Medium

Built entirely in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no frameworks or game engines. Single-file, browser-based, works on any device with no installation required.
